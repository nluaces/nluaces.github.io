whoami

Hello,
I'm Noe Luaces, and among other things, I'm a software engineer by profession.
The purpose of this personal website is sharing my experience and my personal/parallel projects that I've been working on through my career.
In 2007, I finished my 3 year Bachelor Degree in Computer Science, but I always wanted to learn more of subjects that weren't included in my degree; so I enrolled a course to adapt my old degree to 4 year Bachelor Degree while working full time (don't recommend doing both things a the same time though). In that course I've learned about Software Architecture models and techniques, and also I was exposed to functional programming.
I've also worked on initiatives to keep women developers in the industry, by offering them support that I know that is needed (by experience). The reason of that, it's the hostility that remains in some environments towards diverse people in general.

I don't consider myself an expert in other areas, so I will say that I keep insisting on practicing some hobbies through all these years: like drawing, taking pictures with plastic cameras (with plastic lenses, like the one in the photo) or just with my phone, reading and accumulating comics (Japanese, Franco-Belgian or American indie comics), over-analysing indie movies and sometimes, criticising the mainstream ones.
All of these things influence, some how, the rest of my work. I don't work as an illustrator, but I usually create my own drawings to use them in my blog posts.
Another scenario is the one where I use Monkey Island characters' names for mocking in a test, just to have an excuse to suggest to play that game, but only the first two parts.
